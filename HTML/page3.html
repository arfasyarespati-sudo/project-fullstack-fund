The standard keyboard layout, known as QWERTY, was originally designed in the 1870s for mechanical typewriters. It is a common myth that Christopher Sholes created this specific arrangement purely to slow down typists and prevent the physical keys from jamming together.

Instead, the design evolved to separate frequently used letter pairs, allowing the mechanical arms inside the machine to move smoothly without colliding. Despite the rise of modern computers that no longer rely on moving physical parts, the QWERTY layout remains the global standard simply because billions of people have already built the muscle memory to use it.
